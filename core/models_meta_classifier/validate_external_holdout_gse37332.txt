2. VALIDATION DESIGN
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  GSE37332 constitutes the most phylogenetically distant external
  validation dataset available for CRS-AI. Its novelty relative to
  the training corpus is multi-dimensional:

    * Different organism (teleost fish):  CRS-AI was trained on
      Mus musculus liver (GSE11516, GSE11923), Drosophila melanogaster
      clock neurons (GSE77451), and Homo sapiens blood (GSE39445).
      Danio rerio shares the canonical TTFL architecture with mammals
      but diverged ~450 Mya and has undergone extensive teleost-
      specific gene duplication (e.g., per1a/per1b, cry1a/cry1b).

    * Different tissue:  Whole organism vs. liver, sorted neurons,
      and peripheral blood. Whole-body expression integrates signals
      from all tissues, dampening tissue-specific oscillation
      amplitudes. This is expected to be a harder detection task.

    * Different experimental platform:  GPL14664 Agilent custom
      zebrafish 4x44K array (one-color) vs. the Affymetrix and
      Illumina arrays used in training -- different dynamic range,
      background correction, and probe hybridisation chemistry.

    * Zero data leakage:  GSE37332 was never accessed at any step
      of CRS-AI training, hyperparameter optimisation, or feature
      engineering. Labels were assigned entirely from an independent
      BioCycle analysis (RhythmicDB, E-GEOD-37332_LD) after training
      was complete.

  Label quality: BioCycle-consistent non-rhythmic-from-absence
  labeling is used. N-class genes (label=0) carry no BioCycle
  evidence of rhythmicity, while X-class borderline genes are
  fully excluded. This is the same labeling strategy used for
  CRS-AI v6 training on GSE11516, ensuring methodological
  consistency between training labels and validation labels.

5. INTERPRETATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  The AUROC exceeds 0.85, demonstrating strong cross-species
  generalisation of CRS-AI to a teleost species.

  External LODO context: CRS-AI was trained exclusively on
  mouse, Drosophila, and human data. An AUROC of 0.931
  [95% CI 0.907, 0.953] on
  held-out zebrafish data -- a species not seen at any training
  step -- provides direct evidence that the model captures
  organism-agnostic rhythmicity features (waveform shape,
  spectral power, cosinor fit quality) rather than taxon-
  specific expression patterns.

  Label quality is supported by the R-class containing per1a
  (Q=0.000, T=25.01 h), per1b (Q=0.000, T=24.45 h), per3
  (Q=0.029, T=25.50 h), and nr1d1 (Q=0.030, T=23.76 h) --
  components of the canonical zebrafish TTFL -- as well as
  multiple output clock-controlled genes with documented
  rhythmicity in zebrafish.

  Whole-organism caveat: expression from non-oscillating tissues
  dilutes oscillation amplitude for genes with restricted
  circadian expression patterns. The reported AUROC is therefore
  a conservative lower bound on performance achievable with
  tissue-specific zebrafish data (e.g., liver or brain).

  Bootstrap CI width reflects sample size (N=578 instances,
  R=278, N=300). For manuscript reporting, cite the
  CI rather than the point estimate alone.

  Suggested reporting (JBR Methods section):
    "To evaluate cross-species generalisation, CRS-AI was applied
    to GSE37332 (Danio rerio, adult whole organism, LD cycle;
    GPL14664 Agilent microarray; n=578 genes: R=278,
    N=300). Gene labels were derived independently from
    RhythmicDB / BioCycle (E-GEOD-37332_LD; Q<=0.05, period
    20-28 h for rhythmic class; absent-from-database criterion
    for non-rhythmic class). The model achieved
    AUROC=0.931 (95% CI 0.907-0.953) on this zero-overlap external
    dataset, demonstrating generalisation across vertebrate
    phylogenetic distance."
