1. MOTIVATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The CRS-AI Random Forest is trained on features extracted with fixed sub-
method search windows (JTK 20-28h, Lomb-Scargle 18-32h, Cosinor and
Harmonic on a half-step circadian grid 20-28h, F24 target_period=24h,
JTK asymmetry=0.5, harmonic order=2). When the GUI exposes a configurable
`period_range` parameter, the AI module faces a choice: ignore the user
input (silent failure, breaks the UX promise) or honor it (potentially
shifting the feature distribution outside what the model was calibrated
for).

The policy adopted (Section 9 of training_report.txt) honors user
`period_range` only inside the trained window [18, 32]h, clipping if it
spills out and falling back to defaults with a warning if it lies
entirely outside. This file quantifies the cost of that policy on the
v5 holdout.


2. EXPERIMENTAL DESIGN
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - The exact training data and 80/20 GroupShuffleSplit (random_state=42)
    used by train_consensus_model.py are regenerated to recover the same
    683-instance test set.
  - Features are re-extracted over each test instance under two regimes:
        (i)  DEFAULT — training-window settings
        (ii) NARROW — period_range = (22, 26)h, fully inside the
              training window, applied uniformly to JTK / Cosinor /
              LS / Harmonic. F24 target_period and JTK asymmetries
              stay locked (per policy).
  - The same v5 model is used to produce probabilities on each
    feature matrix.
  - Sanity: the re-extracted DEFAULT features reproduce the cached
    X_test.npy bit-for-bit, confirming the test partition is
    deterministic across runs.

5. INTERPRETATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Discrimination is preserved. The 0.009 drop in ROC-AUC lies within the
  95% bootstrap confidence interval of the default holdout AUROC
  ([0.8930, 0.9353]; Section 6.2 of training_report.txt), so the change
  is not statistically distinguishable from sampling noise.

  The cost concentrates on specificity. Narrowing the search window to
  22-26h prevents Lomb-Scargle and Cosinor from reporting periods at
  the edges of the training window (~18-21h, ~27-32h). Non-rhythmic
  genes that previously had a weak spectral peak at, e.g., 20h are now
  forced to report their best period inside 22-26h — closer to 24h on
  average — which raises their amplitude_relative and f24_score and
  pushes them up the probability scale. The result is a 4.4-point
  drop in specificity (15 additional false positives), against a 0.6-
  point gain in recall.

  Calibration also worsens marginally (Brier loss +0.0075) because the
  CalibratedClassifierCV isotonic regression was fit on the default
  feature distribution. Recalibrating per-range would require a
  per-range CV fit at training time; this was judged unnecessary given
  the small magnitude of the shift.

  Most predictions barely move. The median absolute probability shift
  is under 1 percentage point. Only 24 instances out of 683 (3.5%) move
  by more than 20 points, and only 25 cases out of 683 flip their
  binary decision.


6. CONCLUSIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  The bounded `period_range` override policy is empirically safe. The
  specificity cost grows approximately linearly with how much the range
  is narrowed; the (22, 26)h test represents a realistic tight
  narrowing (closer than what most circadian studies require). Users
  who choose narrower ranges are trading global specificity for
  tighter local agreement with their biology — a deliberate trade-off
  the parameter is intended to enable, with the warnings emitted by
  `_resolve_params` making the trade-off explicit.

  Out-of-window ranges (no overlap with [18, 32]h) trigger automatic
  fallback to training defaults with a warning. This case was not
  empirically validated because the policy never lets such inputs
  reach the model; the user is informed and the model behaves
  identically to the baseline.

  n_harmonics override is not validated here because it does not
  affect any feature in the classifier's input vector (harmonic_p_value
  and harmonic_r_squared were dropped in v2). The override only moves
  the UI's per-method panel and is therefore guaranteed not to alter
  predictions.
